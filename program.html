Program

A Doctor's Tragedy

(PG-13)

In the midst of a drug epidemic, the tragedy of death haunts a Doctor who vows to bring back his sister, though that very epidemic seeks to destroy the Doctor's cure.

Director: Frederico Austin

An Andy Chapter

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, harsh language, intense graphic violence, and drug abuse.)

Andy, an addict whose quitting journey has been feeble, faces the comically absurd and ends up finding support in unexpected places.

Director: Kyle Crist

Bottom Out

(PG-13)

Two insecure jocks attempt a bold escape from the underestimated woman they’ve robbed, along the way festering a mutually-assured sexual tension that threatens to boil over.

Director: Trin Moody

- “A queer-centric short that explores the mysterious interconnection of hypermasculinity and homoeroticism when things really get heated. Our queer-dominant cast and crew are what will bring these themes to light.”

Bread

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, and harsh language.)

Dark comedy short follows a heartbroken young man as he delves into betrayal, concealed truths, and family complexities.

Director: Brandon McLamb

Bugman

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes and harsh language.)

A misunderstood and isolated transgender teenager takes revenge upon his unaccepting parents.

Director: Jackson Higgins

The Burn

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, harsh language, and intense graphic violence.)

A hapless 20-something film student has been the secondary character of his life, his whole life. That’s until one fateful day, he’s called into work, only to find a mysterious masked man robbing it. Will he let this ridiculous day pass, like all others, or finally take the reins once and for all?

Director: Jakob Marston-Galarza

- “s/o to the Cheba hut uptown crew”

By Tooth and Maw

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, and intense graphic violence.)

A family's lives are torn apart as a caring parent comes face to face with a horror beyond their comprehension.

Director: Gale Scriven

- “Takes direct inspiration from Dungeons and Dragons”

Contingency

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, harsh language, and intense graphic violence.)

While dealing with an abusive partner Jadyn becomes stuck in a violent situation when she finds out she is pregnant.

Director: Maya Dominguez

- “We are so happy and thankful to everyone who made this film possible! We are so happy to share it with the world!”

Dimensionz

(PG)

In a fateful encounter, Ivan, a courageous 16-year-old, finds himself trapped in an unforgiving forest, drawn to a mysterious cave that holds a secret that should’ve stayed hidden.

Director: Mariana Garcia

Doughboy

Rated-R: Contains adult themes, harsh language, and drug abuse.

Two burntout employees at a locally renowned pizza joint decide to bake a psychedelic mushroom pizza on the clock. When their manager accidentally hands it to a mom and her two small children, the trio must retrieve the pizza before it’s too late, or their reputation will be ruined for good.

Director: Dom Valdez

Fear

Rated-R: Contains adult themes, harsh language, and intense graphic violence.

You Should Have Left

Director: Gaege Sosa

Felinity

(PG)

When a painting mysteriously appears in his apartment one morning, he is thrust into a mystery surrounding what it is to be a vessel for the creative spirit. Max is assisted by his friend Grey as they discover how Max's cat Rutabaga may be the solution to this mysterious painting as well as his guide into the spiritual world of art and creativity.

Director: Matthew Pszyk

- “Got cool puppets”

Forget Me Not

(PG)

A lonely ghost sets out to find an empty gravestone in order not to be forgotten in a cemetery packed with interesting and eclectic spirits.

Director: Daniela Basista

The Garden

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, and harsh language.)

In primordial times God and his angel Uriel struggle with the creation of time and space and the aftermath of accidentally creating humans by heating up Styrofoam in the microwave.

Director: Ted Rivera

Goatheads

(Rated - R: Contains adult themes, harsh language, intense graphic violence, and drug abuse.)

A coming-of-age Albuquerque story about the Ditchgoon skate crew. A visceral adventure in a search to figure out what happened during a crazy party…

Director: Jack Brown

- “Goatheads was written after recovering from a skateboarding accident that ended with a craniotomy… ABQ helped me come back from that. I want to show what that experience is like for any kid trying to make it out of the ditch.”

Goldfish

(PG-13)

Freshly heartbroken, 16-year-old Darren Williams must confront lingering feelings before saying goodbye to his best friend.

Director: Bryant Staff

Gutters & Glory

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, and harsh language.)

After a devastating loss in last years tournament, now senior captain of the bowling club puts together an unenthusiastic new team to compete again.”

Writer: Philip Henshaw

- (we love everyone)

Imogen

(Rated-R: Contains adult themes, and harsh language.)

TRIGGER WARNING: Flashing

When a group of delinquents inadvertently unleashes an evil spirit, they must unearth its intentions and find a path to survival. If that path even exists.

Director: Brayden Palmer

- “Imogen is a film that will bring a visceral new take to the horror genre. The film will pull a lot of inspiration and the best elements in the horror medium, but also one that avoids the exploitation found in the genre and instead delves into the grittier side. Imogen may be the headlining act, but the film's heart will be the characters. The bonds between the characters and the loss/terror they feel will be at the center of the film. All of this poses the question: How far are you willing to go for the ones you love? What exactly will you do to make it happen?”
